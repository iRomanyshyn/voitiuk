# Internet research: Anna Voitiuk / Anna Vojtiuk

## Key findings

1. There is a public Facebook profile labeled **"Anna Vojtiuk (Anna Voitiuk)"**, which strongly suggests both spellings refer to the same person/account (at least in that context).  
   Source: https://www.facebook.com/anna.vojtyuk/photos/

2. Art market pages use the form **Anna Voitiuk** consistently (e.g., Heike Arndt gallery page for "Artprint Anna Voitiuk (UA)").  
   Source: https://webshop.heike-arndt.dk/product/anna-voitiuk-ua-sightseeing-tour/

3. Romanization standards explain why both forms can exist:
   - The official Ukrainian national transliteration (used for passports and official proper names) is English-oriented and tends to produce forms like **Voitiuk**.
   - In Czech/Slavic transliteration contexts, **й** is often represented as **j**, and iotated vowels may be rendered with **ju/ja**, leading to forms like **Vojtiuk**.
   Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_of_Ukrainian

## Practical conclusion

- **Anna Voitiuk** and **Anna Vojtiuk** are likely variant Latin spellings of one Ukrainian surname (Войтюк / Воїтюк-like transliteration context), especially in art/social contexts.
- For formal/official English-language usage, **Voitiuk** is usually safer unless a person explicitly prefers **Vojtiuk** in their own profile.
